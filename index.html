Eidolon Lucky Packs

Eidolon Lucky Packs are used at the Eidolon Den in your house to level up intimacy.

The basic stats gained by intimacy leveling are applied to your character:

  • DMG
  • CRIT
  • SPD
  • EVA
  • HP
  • DEF

Because of this, all Eidolons should be leveled to at least intimacy level 8. It usually takes about 280 Eidolon Lucky Packs to go from level 1 to 8. Level 10 is optional.

Current maximum level 8 intimacy totals based on the current Eidolon count:

  • Eidolons counted: 167
  • DMG: 19873
  • CRIT: 7682
  • SPD: 6346
  • EVA: 10187
  • HP: 24215
  • DEF: 3841

Eidolon Wish Coins

Wish Coins are used to fulfill an Eidolon's wish without needing to gather what they ask for. They are a shortcut to fulfill wishes.

All stats gained this way are applied to your character.

Wish Coin cost per wish level:

  • Wish 1: 1 coin
  • Wish 2: 2 coins
  • Wish 3: 4 coins
  • Wish 4: 8 coins
  • Wish 5: 16 coins
  • Wish 6: 32 coins

All Eidolons should have their wishes fulfilled. This is one of the greatest sources of raw stats.

Holy Chest

Holy Chest Tips

The Holy Chest is a system where you can slot up to 9 gold weapons. The higher their fortification level, the stronger the bonuses you receive. Each weapon has its own Holy Chest bonus, which can be viewed by pressing TAB on the weapon's details.

The Holy Chest is shared across all characters on your account, but the bonuses only apply to characters whose level is equal to or lower than the weapon level inside the chest. This is why using Lv95 Abyss II weapons is recommended ï¿½ they cover all current endgame characters.

  • Early goal ï¿½ +15: Fortify your weapons to at least +15 to unlock the Dragon Points bonus and, for Abyss II weapons, the full elemental damage bonus.
  • Final goal ï¿½ +30: Reach +30 on all 9 weapons to unlock every bonus, including elemental penetration on Abyss II weapons.
  • Best weapons to use: Lv95 Abyss II weapons (Hebe, Cerberus, Izanami, Michaela, Demeter, Hermes). They provide the highest bonuses at both +15 and +30.
  • Best source of elemental damage: Each weapon gives up to 17% damage against a specific element, for a maximum of 153% elemental damage across all 9 slots.
  • Priority elements: Focus on Dark, Lightning, Storm, and Ice ï¿½ these are the main elements needed for Vault of Eternity (floors 8ï¿½12, party).

Upgrading Eidolon skills earns Skill Upgrade Points based on the skill's quality. Only the points of the current highest quality are counted and do not accumulate. Skills from different Eidolons on the same account can be accumulated.

The Total Skill Upgrade Points refer to the sum of the Skill Upgrade Points of all Eidolons in the account. Points for different skills of the same Eidolon are calculated separately.

Gear Guide

This section covers practical crafting priorities for your equipment.

Crafting Notes

  • Your weapon should use your class element because it deals 20% more elemental damage.
  • Your armor element applies only on the chest piece and gives extra defense against that element. Dark element armor is generally preferred.
  • Try to craft at least 120%+ quality on equipment. It is less important on armor, but high weapon quality increases damage noticeably.
  • The quality cap is 130% for Orange equipment and 140% for Gold equipment.

Weapon Progression by Level

The strongest current weapons are farmed in Abyss II for each element. They are the best choices at Lv95, S15, and S35, so after Lv95 you generally do not need other weapon lines.

  • Below Lv95: Use reward weapons from Aura and Advanced Gaia (one from Lv1-40 and one from Lv40-75). They can carry you comfortably until Lv95. For sub get any Nocturnal weapon from NPC.
  • Lv95: Craft the Abyss II weapon for your element: Hebe (Ice), Cerberus (Fire), Izanami (Dark), Michaela (Holy), Demeter (Storm), Hermes (Lightning). For physical builds, pick the weapon that gives the best boost to your main skills. If you do not want Abyss II yet, use Lv95 gold with your element or craft Lv90 orange with your preferred core. For sub weapon craft lvl 90 Nocturnal, usually bard one.
  • S5: If you still do not have a Lv95 option, craft your Abyss II element weapon or use an S5 gold weapon with your element. If you crafted lvl90 Nocturnal sub, just keep it. If you didn't, craft either lvl90 or S1.
  • S10: The Lv95 Abyss II weapon is still good here. If you really want to change, craft the S10 orange weapon. You can craft S10 sub Nocturnal weapon if you wish to.
  • S15: Craft the S15 Abyss II weapon for your element. This is your best weapon line until S35.
  • S20: (Optional) Craft Nocturnal sub weapon of your choice (usually bard if you need detail and healing or shuriken if you have enough detail).
  • S30: You can craft S30 orange if you want to, but I recommend sticking with S15 Abyss II. Craft Nocturnal sub weapon of your choice (usually bard if you need detail and healing or shuriken if you have enough detail).
  • S35: You can switch to S35 gold for your element, but it is optional. The best weapon is still Abyss II S35 of your element; S35 Abyss II is difficult to farm, so swap when you are strong enough and can farm it consistently.

Weapon Core Options

  • Destroyer: 10% DEF Shred.
  • Nocturnal: 3% Absorb DMG to HP. This is nerfed in some dungeons.
  • Deadly: 15% CRIT DMG.
  • Restorer (Bard Only): 10% Heal.

Armor / Trophy / Accessories Core Options

  • Imperial: 3% Move SPD.
  • Blessed: 5% EXP Gain.
  • Bestial: 1% DMG + 1% HP.
  • Spiky: 1% DMG + 1% DEF.

Mount Buff

  • 40% bonus to your class element skill damage.

Weapon Secret Stone

Always get the secret stone for your class skill.

  • Piercing Secret Stone ï¿½ Best in slot, but expensive.
  • Lava Secret Stone ï¿½ Cheaper than Piercing, and can be farmed in Pyroclastic Purgatory.
  • Orange Class Master Stone ï¿½ Use as a placeholder before getting Lava or better.

Armor Secret Stone

Purchase them at your class Master in Navea. They will be orange ï¿½ don't worry about the stats. Level them to 70, then upgrade to purple to add one more stat, and they are ready to reroll.

Best Stats to Aim For (Rerolling)
  • Detail-DMG (or DMG): +5 / +4 / +3
  • CRIT DMG: +10 / +8 / +6

Always aim for Detail-DMG +5% or at least DMG +4%. The most common strategy is to get Detail-DMG +5 on the last line, then use a DMG + Something reroll potion to aim for double Detail-DMG on the stone.

With enough economy, you can push further and get a stone with three damage stats ï¿½ for example: The DMG caused is increased by 6% / DMG +3% / Detail-DMG +5%.

Boost your damage

This guide focuses on practical ways to increase your overall damage output.

Back Strike

Back Strike means attacking enemies from behind. Doing this grants 50% amplified damage.

Whenever possible, reposition to stay behind the target and maintain this bonus.

Jump Casting

Jump Casting is a combat mechanic where you jump and then cast your skills. This helps cancel or shorten many animations that would normally lock you in place.

It also weaves basic attacks between skills, which increases your total damage output over time.

Eidolon Stars

Each Eidolon can have up to 4 star bonuses, unlocked by leveling and evolving the Eidolon.

Stars 1 and 2 provide lower-tier bonuses, while stars 3 and 4 provide higher-tier bonuses.

For star stats, the standard recommendation is to keep 1 lower-tier and 1 higher-tier damage bonus for your element. The other 2 bonuses should be selected based on your build and current stats.

Case 1: Carry all dungeons and VoE 1-9

If Total CDMG is 40+ below Cap CDMG:

  • X (lower-tier): 20 CDMG / 8% DMG / 5% Double Attack
  • Y (higher-tier): 40 CDMG / 16% DMG / 10% Double Attack

If CDMG is at Cap CDMG:

  • X (lower-tier): 8% DMG / 5% Double Attack / 15% DMG to elementals
  • Y (higher-tier): 16% DMG / 10% Double Attack / 30% DMG to elementals

Case 2: Carry VoE 1-12

If Total CDMG is 40+ below Cap CDMG:

  • X (lower-tier): 20 CDMG / 8% DMG / 5% Double Attack
  • Y (higher-tier): 40 CDMG / 10% Double Attack / 8% DMG

If CDMG is at Cap CDMG:

  • X (lower-tier): 5% Double Attack / 15% DMG to elementals / 8% DMG
  • Y (higher-tier): 10% Double Attack / 30% DMG to elementals / 16% DMG

Examples

If CDMG is not close to cap:

  • Star 1: Fire Skill Damage 15%
  • Star 2: Crit Damage +20%
  • Star 3: Fire Skill Damage +30%
  • Star 4: Crit Damage +40%

If CDMG is close to cap:

  • Star 1: Fire Skill Damage 15%
  • Star 2: Double Attack 5%
  • Star 3: Fire Skill Damage +30%
  • Star 4: Double Attack 10%

Notes: Total CDMG = Primary CDMG + CDMG against bosses.

If your CDMG is only 20 to 30 below Cap CDMG, use X from Case 2.1 and Y from Case 2.2.
